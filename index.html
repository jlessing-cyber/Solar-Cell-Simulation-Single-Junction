The Shockley–Queisser Limit
A single-junction silicon cell can convert at most ~33% of sunlight into electricity. Photons below the bandgap are wasted as heat; those above it lose excess energy to thermalization.
💡
Why Multi-Junction?
Stacking junctions with different bandgaps captures a wider slice of the solar spectrum. Concentrator multi-junction cells have exceeded 47% efficiency in lab conditions.
🌐
What is Stopping Solar?
Physics sets hard limits, but manufacturing cost, material scarcity, grid integration, and energy storage remain the practical barriers to wider global solar adoption.
